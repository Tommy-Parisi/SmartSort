# FileSort — Install Guide

**Sort thousands of files into smart folders in one click. No setup required.**

---

## Download & Install (Mac)

1. **Download** `FileSort.dmg` from [gumroad.com/l/filesort](https://gumroad.com/l/filesort)
2. **Open** the `.dmg` file (double-click it in your Downloads folder)
3. **Drag** the FileSort icon into your Applications folder
4. **Open** FileSort from your Applications folder or Launchpad

> **"FileSort can't be opened because it's from an unidentified developer"?**
> Right-click the app icon → click **Open** → click **Open** again. You only need to do this once.

---

## Sort Your Files

1. Click **Choose Folder** (or drag a folder onto the window)
2. FileSort scans your files and shows a preview of the folders it will create
3. Click **Sort** to move everything into organized folders
4. Done — your files are sorted

That's it. FileSort works entirely on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.

## Activate Pro

After purchasing, you'll receive a license key by email.

1. Open FileSort
2. Click the **⚙ Settings** icon (top right)
3. Click **Enter License Key**
4. Paste your key and click **Activate**

Your license is stored on your Mac. No account or internet connection needed after activation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does FileSort move my files or copy them?**
It moves them — your originals go into the new sorted folders. Nothing is deleted and nothing is duplicated.

**Can I undo a sort?**
Not automatically yet. If you want to be safe, sort a copy of your folder first.

**Does it work on external drives or iCloud folders?**
Yes — any folder you can see in Finder will work.

**My files didn't sort the way I expected. Why?**
FileSort reads the content of your documents to decide where they belong. Files with very little text (images, videos, empty docs) go into an Unsorted folder so nothing gets lost.

**Is my data private?**
Yes. FileSort runs entirely on your Mac. Your files and their contents never leave your computer.

**I have more than 500 files. Do I need Pro?**
Only if you want to sort them all at once. In trial mode you can sort any folder — FileSort will let you know if it has more than 500 files and ask you to upgrade.
